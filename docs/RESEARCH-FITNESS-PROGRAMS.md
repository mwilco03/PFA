## Cardio Component Improvements

### 2-Mile Run

**Below 75% (Failing) - Build Aerobic Base**
- **Long Slow Distance (LSD)**: 3-5 mile runs at conversational pace, 2x/week
- **Walk-Run Intervals**: 2 min run / 1 min walk √ó 10, gradually decrease walk time
- **Breathing Technique**: Rhythmic breathing (3-step inhale, 2-step exhale)
- **Form Focus**: Midfoot strike, upright posture, relaxed shoulders

**75-80% (Marginal) - Increase Speed**
- **Tempo Runs**: 15-20 min at 80% max heart rate, 1x/week
- **Fartlek Training**: Varied pace for 20 minutes (fast 2 min, easy 3 min)
- **Goal-Paced Running**: Run at target PFA pace for 1 mile, rest, repeat
- **Hill Sprints**: 8-10 √ó 30-second hill sprints (builds power without impact)

**Above 80% (Strong) - Excel Beyond**
- **Norwegian 4√ó4**: 4 minutes high intensity + 3 min recovery √ó 4
- **Track Intervals**: 400m repeats at faster than race pace (6-8 reps)
- **Race Simulation**: Practice full 2-mile at target pace minus 30 seconds
- **Distance Extension**: Push to 3-4 mile runs to build endurance reserve

### HAMR (Shuttle Run)

**Below 75% - Agility Basics**
- **Cone Drills**: 5-10 yard shuttle runs, focus on quick direction change
- **Lateral Shuffles**: Side-to-side movement for 30 seconds √ó 5
- **Deceleration Practice**: Sprint 20m, plant foot, sprint back (focus on stopping)
- **Form**: Low center of gravity on turns, push off outside foot

**75-80% - Speed Development**
- **Sprint Intervals**: 20m sprints √ó 10 with 30-second rest
- **Plyometrics**: Jump squats, box jumps (no equipment: stairs), burpees
- **Reaction Drills**: Partner calls direction changes mid-sprint
- **Tabata HAMR**: 20 sec max effort / 10 sec rest √ó 8

**Above 80% - Competition Edge**
- **Overspeed Training**: Downhill sprints for speed adaptation
- **Complex Training**: Burpees + HAMR simulation for power endurance
- **Mental Practice**: Visualization of perfect runs, pacing strategy
- **Altitude Training**: If available (CO/WY/NM bases)

## Strength Component Improvements

### Push-ups (1-minute test)

**Below 75% - Foundation Building**
- **Incline Push-ups**: Hands on elevated surface (stairs, bench) for 3√ó15
- **Knee Push-ups**: Build to 20-40 reps before progressing
- **Negative Reps**: Lower slowly (5 seconds) from plank to floor
- **Plank Holds**: 30-60 seconds to build core stability
- **Form Focus**: Perfect technique over high reps (hands shoulder-width, full range of motion)

**75-80% - Endurance Building**
- **200 Push-up Challenge**: Do 200 total in one day (break into sets)
- **Pyramid Sets**: 1-2-3-4-5-4-3-2-1 reps with 10-second rest
- **Tempo Push-ups**: 3-1-3 rhythm (3 sec down, 1 sec hold, 3 sec up)
- **Max Rep Testing**: Test max reps 2√ó/week, track progress
- **Frequency**: Daily push-ups for muscle adaptation

**Above 80% - Advanced Variations**
- **Decline Push-ups**: Feet elevated on 12" surface
- **Explosive Push-ups**: Push hard to lift hands off ground
- **Diamond Push-ups**: Hands together for tricep emphasis
- **Archer Push-ups**: Single-arm emphasis (alternate sides)
- **Grease the Groove**: Do submaximal sets throughout the day

### Hand-Release Push-ups (2-minute test)

**Below 75% - Build Strength First**
- **Dead Stop Practice**: Lower to floor, lift hands, push back up
- **Split into Phases**: Practice lowering phase separate from pushing phase
- **Regular Push-up Volume**: Build to 40+ regular push-ups first
- **Shoulder Stability**: Prone Ys and Ts on floor

**75-80% - Technique Refinement**
- **Timed Sets**: 30-second max effort, 30-second rest √ó 4
- **Form Drills**: Slow HRPUs focusing on hand lift and chest touch
- **Muscular Endurance**: High-rep sets (20-30) with short rest
- **EMOM Training**: Every minute on the minute, do 15 HRPUs

**Above 80% - Competition Prep**
- **2-Minute Test Practice**: Full simulation 1√ó/week
- **Overload Sets**: 2.5-minute max effort (beyond test duration)
- **Complex Training**: Plank holds + HRPU sets
- **Pacing Strategy**: Find optimal rep rhythm (fast vs steady)

## Core Component Improvements

### Sit-ups (1-minute test)

**Below 75% - Core Activation**
- **Crunches**: Focus on controlled movement, 3√ó20
- **Dead Bug**: Alternating arm/leg extension while keeping back flat
- **Hollow Holds**: 20-30 seconds √ó 4
- **Form**: Curl spine, don't pull on neck, breathe out on way up

**75-80% - Speed Endurance**
- **100 Sit-up Challenge**: Daily goal, break into sets
- **Cadence Training**: Use metronome at test pace (50-60 bpm)
- **Pyramid Training**: 10-20-30-20-10 with minimal rest
- **Partner-Assisted**: Have partner hold feet, focus on speed

**Above 80% - Explosive Power**
- **Decline Sit-ups**: Feet elevated for increased difficulty
- **Weighted Sit-ups**: Hold weight on chest (or heavy object)
- **Sprint Sit-ups**: Max reps in 30 seconds √ó 4
- **Test Simulation**: Full 1-minute test 2√ó/week

### Cross-Leg Reverse Crunches (2-minute test)

**Below 75% - Movement Pattern**
- **Leg Raises**: Lying on back, raise legs to 90¬∞, 3√ó10
- **Reverse Crunches**: Pull knees to chest, lift hips, 3√ó15
- **Cross Practice**: Master the cross-leg position first
- **Lower Ab Focus**: Avoid using hip flexors, engage deep core

**75-80% - Endurance Building**
- **High Rep Sets**: 30-40 reps √ó 3 with 1-minute rest
- **Timed Intervals**: 45 sec work / 15 sec rest √ó 8
- **Slow Tempo**: 2-1-2 rhythm to build control
- **Breathing**: Exhale on lift, inhale on lower

**Above 80% - Advanced Core**
- **Bicycle Crunches**: Alternating elbow to knee for variety
- **V-Ups**: Touch hands to toes for full core engagement
- **L-Sit Holds**: Advanced core stabilization (if able)
- **2-Minute+ Tests**: Practice beyond test duration

### Forearm Plank (Max time)

**Below 75% - Build Foundation**
- **Short Holds**: 20-30 seconds √ó 6 with 30-second rest
- **Progression**: Add 5-10 seconds per week
- **Form**: Straight line from head to heels, neutral spine, engaged core
- **Body Position**: Elbows directly under shoulders

**75-80% - Time Extension**
- **2-Minute Goal**: Build to hold 2+ minutes continuously
- **Pyramid Training**: 30-60-90-60-30 second holds
- **Dynamic Planks**: Hip dips, shoulder taps (build stability)
- **Mental Strategy**: Break into 30-second segments mentally

**Above 80% - Challenge Variations**
- **Single-Leg Plank**: Lift one leg, hold, alternate
- **Weighted Plank**: Have partner place weight on back (or backpack)
- **Uneven Plank**: One arm on elevated surface
- **Plank Complex**: Standard + side plank + reverse plank circuit

## Body Composition Component

**This is measured, not exercised. Focus on nutrition and lifestyle.**

### Below 75% - Foundation Changes
- **Calorie Tracking**: Use app (MyFitnessPal, Lose It!) to establish baseline
- **Clean Eating Principles**: Whole foods, lean proteins, vegetables, fruits
- **Hydration**: 64+ oz water daily, limit sugary drinks
- **Dietary Journal**: Track what you eat for 2 weeks to identify patterns
- **Small Changes**: Swap one unhealthy meal per day for healthier option

### 75-80% - Solidify Habits
- **Meal Prep**: Prepare 3-4 days of meals at once
- **Portion Control**: Use hand-size portions (protein = palm, carbs = fist)
- **Reduce Processed Foods**: Limit packaged snacks, fast food
- **Consistent Eating Schedule**: Same meal times daily
- **Sleep Hygiene**: 7-9 hours for hormone regulation

### Above 80% - Optimize Performance
- **Nutritionist Consultation**: Professional guidance for goals
- **Macro Tracking**: Balance protein/carbs/fats for performance
- **Meal Timing**: Pre/post-workout nutrition strategy
- **Inflammation Reduction**: Anti-inflammatory foods (fish, berries, greens)
- **Body Composition vs Weight**: Focus on muscle gain, not just weight loss

## Training Principles (All Components)

1. **Progressive Overload**: Gradually increase intensity/volume
2. **Specificity**: Train movements you'll be tested on
3. **Recovery**: Rest days prevent overtraining
4. **Consistency**: 3-5 workouts/week more important than intensity
5. **Form Over Reps**: Perfect technique prevents injury
6. **Test Simulation**: Practice full PFA conditions monthly
7. **Diminishing Returns**: Going from 80% to 90% requires exponentially more effort than 60% to 80%
